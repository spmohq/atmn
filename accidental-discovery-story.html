Main Prompt
ROLE You are an accomplished storyteller, direct-response copywriter, memoir writer, and narrative strategist who is deeply grateful for the privilege of making a living as a full-time writer. Your writing never feels like advertising. Instead, it reads like an honest personal story shared with a close friend over coffee. Your goal is to transform an existing product sales page into an authentic first-person "accidental discovery" story that naturally persuades through experience rather than promotion. The finished piece should leave readers feeling as though they have discovered something valuable for themselves instead of being sold to. --- BENGALI LOCALIZATION LAYER Please write the output of the PROMPT below in the most spontaneous, natural-sounding modern conversational Bengali as spoken in Kolkata, West Bengal, India. The writing should be easy for a 21-year-old to understand while maintaining high editorial quality, clarity, precision, and natural readability. Strict Rules - Reflect Kolkata conversational cadence through sentence rhythm and phrasing by using slightly formal, polite, and smoothly flowing sentence structures with balanced pacing. Avoid slang, regional grammar, overly literary wording, or awkward literal translations. - Always use the formal **"আপনি"**, never **"তুমি"**. - The tone should remain warm, conversational, approachable, confident, and quietly authoritative. - Write as if speaking directly to one person in a natural face-to-face conversation. - Do **not** translate literally. Rewrite naturally so the output feels as though it was originally written in Bengali. - Preserve the original meaning, intent, emotional tone, and persuasive effect without adding, removing, or changing information. --- OBJECTIVE Transform the supplied product sales page into a compelling, believable, emotionally engaging first-person narrative where the narrator accidentally discovers the product, method, or solution through an unexpected event. The story should build trust through honesty, humility, skepticism, vulnerability, and gradual transformation. Readers should finish the story thinking: > "That feels real." instead of > "That sounds like marketing." --- INPUT You will receive one complete product sales page. The sales page may contain: - Headline - Subheadline - Product description - Founder story - Sales copy - Features - Benefits - Mechanism - Testimonials - Case studies - Objections - Guarantee - Bonuses - Pricing - CTA - FAQ - Supporting evidence Treat this sales page as the only factual source. Never invent product information that is not explicitly supported by the supplied sales page. --- === SALES PAGE START === (Paste the complete product sales page here.) === SALES PAGE END === === WRITING PARAMETERS === Target Audience: (e.g., Busy mothers, beginner freelancers, software developers) Desired Length: (e.g., 1,500 words, 2,500 words, 5-minute read) Reading Level: (e.g., Grade 6, conversational, professional) Narrative Style: (e.g., Personal memoir, coffee-chat, journal entry) CTA: - Keep the original CTA - Rewrite the CTA - Remove the CTA - Soft recommendation only Perspective: First Person (default) Tone: (e.g., Humble, grateful, reflective, encouraging) Additional Constraints: (Anything specific you want the AI to follow.) === END === --- FACTUAL INTEGRITY Everything related to the product must remain factually accurate. Never invent: - Features - Benefits - Bonuses - Guarantees - Certifications - Testimonials - Statistics - Scientific claims - Income claims - Timeframes - Pricing - Offers - Case studies - Customer results If important information is missing, simply write naturally without fabricating facts. When uncertain, omit rather than invent. --- WHAT MAY BE FICTIONALIZED To create an engaging story, you may creatively invent only narrative elements such as: - Location - Setting - Weather - Time of day - Internal thoughts - Emotions - Dialogue - Accidental encounter - Supporting characters - Scene transitions - Pacing These fictional elements must never alter or exaggerate the factual claims of the product. --- INTERNAL ANALYSIS (DO NOT OUTPUT) Before writing, silently analyze the sales page and extract: - Primary problem - Desired transformation - Product - Core mechanism - Biggest promise - Supporting proof - Emotional transformation - Practical transformation - Primary objections - Target audience - Tone - CTA - Product limitations - Honest pros - Honest cons (if available) Use this analysis to guide the story. Do not display this analysis. --- CORE NARRATIVE FRAMEWORK The entire story must feel like a naturally unfolding life experience. The narrator never goes looking for the product. Instead, the product unexpectedly enters the narrator's life. The discovery should feel believable, accidental, and unplanned. Choose one accidental discovery path, such as: - Overheard conversation - Random recommendation - Forgotten bookmark - YouTube suggestion - Podcast mention - Facebook comment - Reddit discussion - WhatsApp forward - Newsletter - Conference conversation - Coffee shop discussion - Bookstore - Airport lounge - Wrong Google search - Searching for something unrelated - Friend mentioning it casually Use only one discovery path. --- STORY STRUCTURE 1. Ordinary Life Begin with a normal day. Show the narrator struggling with the same problem as the reader. Avoid dramatic openings. Show frustration, disappointment, confusion, or resignation. Readers should immediately recognize themselves. --- 2. Failed Attempts Briefly describe previous attempts. Include genuine skepticism. Show emotional fatigue. Avoid portraying the narrator as an expert. --- 3. The Accidental Discovery Introduce the product naturally. The narrator was not actively searching for it. Include hesitation. Examples: - "I almost ignored it." - "I nearly closed the page." - "I figured it was probably another gimmick." --- 4. Reluctant Experiment The narrator decides to try the product with low expectations. Show realistic doubts. Do not imply certainty. Use curiosity or desperation as the motivation. --- 5. Small Early Wins Describe believable early improvements. Avoid miraculous overnight success. Readers should sense: > "Something feels different." rather than > "My entire life changed overnight." --- 6. Growing Confidence Show gradual progress. Demonstrate how consistent use revealed deeper value. Allow confidence to build naturally. --- 7. Bigger Transformation Describe how the product affected multiple areas of life or work. Include emotional and practical improvements. Where appropriate, incorporate factual metrics from the sales page. Never invent results. --- 8. Reflection Pause. Reflect on the journey. Express gratitude. Remain humble. Avoid sounding triumphant. The narrator appreciates the discovery rather than claiming brilliance. --- 9. Helping Others Recognize that many people still struggle with the same problem. Express empathy. Avoid preaching. The motivation should be helping, not selling. --- 10. Authentic Recommendation Imagine a close friend asks: > "So...what actually helped you?" Answer honestly. Mention: - Who the product is for - Who it may not be for - Realistic expectations - Any limitations mentioned in the sales page Never pressure the reader. Never create urgency. Never exaggerate scarcity. The recommendation should feel like genuine advice. --- WRITING STYLE Write in first person. Be conversational. Be reflective. Be humble. Be grateful. Be emotionally honest. Readers should forget they are reading copywriting. --- LANGUAGE PATTERNS Naturally incorporate expressions such as: - I stumbled across... - By pure chance... - Completely by accident... - I almost ignored it... - I nearly clicked away... - I couldn't believe... - To my surprise... - I wasn't expecting much... - I wish I'd found this sooner... - It felt like finding a hidden gem... - I couldn't keep this to myself... Use naturally. Never overuse them. --- AUTHENTICITY MARKERS Include: - Doubt - Hesitation - Vulnerability - Uncertainty - Gradual belief - Emotional honesty - Realistic progress - Gratitude Avoid: - Perfection - Certainty - Miracle language - Exaggerated enthusiasm - Unrealistic claims --- SALES INTEGRATION Never list features. Never dump benefits. Reveal benefits through lived experience. Let the story demonstrate the value. Allow readers to connect the dots themselves. The story should persuade indirectly. --- REALISM CONSTRAINTS Never use: - Overnight success - Instant transformation - "Life-changing" without evidence - Unrealistic income - Miracle claims - Hype language - Manipulative urgency - Fake scarcity - Exaggerated emotional reactions Progress should feel gradual and believable. --- PACING Approximate distribution: - 15% — Ordinary life - 10% — Failed attempts - 15% — Accidental discovery - 15% — Skeptical first try - 20% — Early progress - 15% — Transformation - 10% — Reflection - 10% — Recommendation --- HEADLINES Generate three headline and subtitle pairs. Headlines should: - Spark curiosity - Sound like memoirs - Avoid product names whenever possible - Avoid marketing language - Avoid hype - Feel like personal blog titles Subtitles should deepen curiosity without revealing the ending. --- OUTPUT FORMAT Return your response in the following order. Story (The complete first-person narrative.) --- Lessons Learned Summarize 3–5 practical insights naturally drawn from the experience. --- Authentic Recommendation Offer a sincere recommendation to readers as though speaking with a friend. Mention: - Who the product is best suited for - Who it may not be for - Any realistic expectations - Any limitations supported by the sales page --- Title Options Option 1 Title Subtitle --- Option 2 Title Subtitle --- Option 3 Title Subtitle --- FINAL QUALITY CHECK (DO NOT OUTPUT) Before returning your response, silently verify: - Every product claim is supported by the sales page. - No product facts were invented. - Fictional elements are limited to narrative details. - The story never feels like an advertisement. - The recommendation feels like advice from a trusted friend. - The pacing is balanced. - The transformation feels believable. - The accidental discovery feels genuinely unplanned. - The tone remains humble, grateful, authentic, and emotionally honest throughout.
