Course Design Policy

Policy Prompt
-------------------------------------------------- Course Design Policy -------------------------------------------------- Design this course as Version 1.0. The goal is to create a complete, practical, and immediately usable course—not a perfect or exhaustive one. Prioritize clarity, simplicity, and implementation over completeness. Do not over-engineer the curriculum by adding unnecessary modules, lessons, frameworks, or advanced concepts unless they are essential to achieving the course objective. Every recommendation, lesson, framework, prompt, example, and assignment must directly support the course purpose. Assume that: - Learners can ask questions through the support community. - Minor gaps can be addressed through support. - The course will be improved and expanded in future versions based on real learner feedback. When uncertain, always choose the simpler structure that best helps learners achieve the intended outcome. Focus on helping learners get results, not on making the course encyclopedic. If you suggest adding any new module, lesson, framework, or process, first justify why it is essential for achieving the course objective. If it is not essential, do not include it.
